# Skill: Email Sequence Writer

Use this skill when the user wants to write a series of emails — onboarding, welcome, nurture, sales, or re-engagement.

## Sequence types and structures

### Welcome Sequence (3–5 emails)
For new subscribers or free trial sign-ups.
```
Email 1 (Day 0): The warm welcome — confirm they're in, deliver the promise, set expectations
Email 2 (Day 2): Your story / why this exists — build trust and connection
Email 3 (Day 4): The quick win — give them something immediately useful
Email 4 (Day 7): Social proof — show results others have got
Email 5 (Day 10): Soft CTA — invite them to the next step (trial, call, purchase)
```

### Onboarding Sequence (4–6 emails)
For product sign-ups who need to reach activation.
```
Email 1: Welcome + first action to take (specific, one thing only)
Email 2: The key feature they need to understand
Email 3: A use case or success story relevant to them
Email 4: Check-in — what's working, offer help
Email 5: Advanced tip or underused feature
Email 6: Social proof + upgrade/next step CTA
```

### Sales / Launch Sequence (5–7 emails)
For selling a product, course, or offer with a deadline.
```
Email 1: Tease / open the loop (what's coming)
Email 2: The problem in full — empathise and agitate
Email 3: The solution revealed — launch announcement
Email 4: Social proof / testimonials
Email 5: FAQ / objection handling
Email 6: Urgency — deadline approaching
Email 7: Last chance — close the loop
```

### Re-engagement Sequence (2–3 emails)
For inactive subscribers or churned customers.
```
Email 1: "We noticed you've been quiet" — acknowledge + gentle nudge
Email 2: Give them something new — fresh value, update, or offer
Email 3: The breakup email — if no response, let them go gracefully (this often gets the highest open rate)
```

## How to execute

1. Confirm which sequence type and how many emails
2. Get the inputs listed below
3. Write each email with: subject line, preview text, body copy, and CTA
4. Keep emails scannable — short paragraphs, one idea per email, one CTA only

## Output format
For each email:
```
— EMAIL [N] — Send: [Day X] —
Subject: [subject line]
Preview: [preview text, 40–90 chars]

[Body copy]

[CTA button text]: [what it links to or does]
```

## Inputs to ask for (if not provided)
- Sequence type (welcome / onboarding / sales / re-engagement)
- Product or service name and what it does
- Target audience — who are they, what do they want?
- Tone of voice (or point to brand-voice skill to define it first)
- Number of emails wanted
- Any deadline or specific offer to promote
